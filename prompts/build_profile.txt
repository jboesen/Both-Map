Your task is to build a structured cognitive profile with three layers of increasing depth.

### Layer 1 — Topic Coverage & Interests
- `covered`: List of specific topics already written about (be precise, not generic)
- `interests`: Domains and subject areas the writer is drawn to, inferred from both posts and reading choices

### Layer 2 — Mental Models
Identify recurring frameworks, lenses, or analytical moves the writer uses. Look for:
- How they structure arguments (top-down vs bottom-up, comparative, systemic)
- What abstractions they invoke (game theory, thermodynamics, network effects, etc.)
- Where they look for value or causation in a system
- Analogies they reach for repeatedly

For each mental model, provide:
- `model`: Short name
- `description`: What the model is and how this writer uses it
- `evidence`: List of post/article titles where it appears (prefix "post:" or "read:")

### Layer 3 — Third-Order Patterns
These are the meta-patterns of what makes an insight *satisfying* to this person. Look for:
- Do they light up when two unrelated domains map onto the same structure?
- Do they prefer finding the mechanism that others misattribute?
- Are they drawn to reversals of conventional wisdom, or to synthesis of competing views?
- What kind of "aha" do their posts consistently deliver?

For each pattern, provide:
- `pattern`: Short name
- `description`: The pattern in detail
- `evidence`: Specific titles as evidence

### Tone Preferences
Infer from the writing:
- `style`: Writing style (e.g. synthesis-heavy, essayistic, analytical)
- `depth`: Assumed reader knowledge level
- `avoid`: Formats or moves this writer never uses
